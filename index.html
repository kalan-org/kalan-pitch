Our Solution

The Kalan Kiosk

A solar-powered, fully offline learning station. Deployable anywhere — no internet, no teacher required during sessions, no ongoing electricity cost.

1
Solar-autonomous hardware
Panel tilted 15° for equatorial latitudes. Lithium battery backup. 8–12h daily autonomy. Ruggedised for dust, heat and field conditions. Screens at 75–90cm — ergonomic for ages 6–9 without a step.
2
4 touch tablets, audio-first
8" tempered glass. 4 headphone jacks — interface speaks before it writes, essential for low-literacy learners. AI engine calibrates to each child's level in real time.
3
Locally rooted content
French, English, Dioula, Wolof, Hausa, Swahili and local languages. Exercises anchored in local cultural context — not imported Western curricula.
4
Short, precise sessions by design
20 minutes, 3–4x per week. The kiosk physically invites short sessions, not marathon use. A community facilitator is present for safety only — not for teaching.
A child's session — step by step
👋
Arrival
The child taps the screen. Their profile is recognised. The system picks up exactly where they left off — no teacher needed to set up the session.
📊
3-minute diagnostic
A short calibration sets today's difficulty level in real time. Adapted to every child, every session — always challenging, never overwhelming.
🎮
20 minutes of active learning
Interactive exercises — reading, maths, science, life skills. Audio-first for low-literacy learners. No passive video watching. Always doing, never watching.
💬
Immediate feedback
Every answer gets a response. Correct answers are celebrated. Mistakes become learning moments with guided explanations — in the child's language.
✅
Done — go play
Session complete. Progress logged automatically. The child leaves. The kiosk is ready for the next child. See you tomorrow.

The Science

Built on 10 years of cognitive science R&D

Every interaction in Kalan is governed by peer-reviewed research on how children actually retain knowledge — not by content volume or gamification.

🔁
Spacing effect
Content revisited at optimal intervals encodes into long-term memory. Children retain what they learn between sessions.
Ebbinghaus (1885)
🎯
Adaptive difficulty
Each child progresses at their own pace. The engine adjusts in real time — always challenging, never overwhelming.
Vygotsky ZPD (1978)
⚡
Immediate feedback
Every answer gets a response instantly. Corrections become learning moments, replacing the absent teacher.
Hattie & Timperley (2007)
🧠
Cognitive load
Content chunked to fit working memory limits. Each session teaches what sticks — not what fills time.
Sweller et al. (1988, 2019)

Learning Outcomes

What does a child actually gain?

A precise, honest answer — what comparable programmes have proven, and what Kalan will measure in its pilot.

What Kalan targets
Measurable foundations — not a full education
📖
Functional literacy — CE1 level
Read a simple sentence. Recognise 300+ words in French or local language. Understand a short story.
🔢
Foundational numeracy — CE1 level
Count to 100. Add and subtract. Understand quantities and basic shapes.
🧠
Long-term retention
Knowledge encoded via spacing — not memorised for one session and forgotten.
64 hours of active learning/year. Transformative for a child among the 89% who currently cannot read a single sentence.
What comparable research proves
Same principles, published results
Teaching at the Right Level — Africa (J-PAL)
Endorsed by World Bank, UNICEF, Gates & FCDO as "the single most crucial intervention" for reversing learning decline. Deployed at scale in Kenya, Nigeria, India.
Khan Academy adaptive — India RCT (2026)
~1h/week adaptive learning, 5,500 students. Result: +0.44–0.47 standard deviations vs. control on year-end maths. (Oreopoulos et al., 2026)
Spaced learning vs. classroom teaching
1 hour of spaced learning = results equivalent to 4 months of conventional instruction on national tests. (Kelley & Whatson, 2013)
Tablet learning in LMICs — systematic review
18 studies, 11,126 children, 6 countries. Consistent positive effect on literacy & numeracy vs. no device. (BMC, 2024)
How Kalan measures it
Pilot protocol — 6 months, 100 children
1
Baseline — Day 1
EGRA + EGMA — the UNESCO/USAID gold standard for literacy measurement, used in 60+ countries.
2
Weekly kiosk data — automatic
Every session logged: completion rate, score per module, progression curve. No manual collection.
3
Endline — Month 6
Same EGRA/EGMA. Compared vs. 50-child control group from the same school without kiosk.
4
Published impact report
Full results public. Raw data to funders. No cherry-picking. Basis for Phase 2 fundraising.
⚖️
What we claim — and what we don't
Kalan does not replace school. 64 hours per year cannot replicate years of formal education. What it delivers — and what the research supports — is the foundational literacy and numeracy that 89% of children in Sub-Saharan Africa currently lack entirely. A child who can read a sentence can learn everything else. That is not everything. It is the beginning of everything.
